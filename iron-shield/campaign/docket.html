Background Information

Malden

Geography & Population

Malden is Mediterranean Micro-Nation, population of 560,000, formed from the major island of Malden, with 6 smaller outlying islands.

The largest outlying island was used as a Military Training area, and is now abandoned. The next-largest outlying island is home to the Pegasus Air Company, a minor airstrip.

Economy

The micronation is classed as a LEDC, with an economy based on farming and some tourism. However the economy has been artificially boosted through foreign military training on the island, most recently though the lease of a number of lands to a PMC.

Despite the boosts from the foreign militaries, both through direct payments to the Government, and indirect payments to locals for food and services, poverty is rife.

Politics & Legal System

Malden is a de-jure constitutional democratic republic, with conjoined executive and legislative branches, with an independent judiciary. The Executive branch (Government) is known as the Ruling Council, the Legislative branch is known as the General Assembly.

Elections are held every 2 years, with individual seats held for 6 years. The Ruling Council is formed from the ruling party / coalition, with the Council being formed of:

  • The President of the State (Leader of the Executive)
  • Minister of the Budget (Budget, Taxation Policy, Joint Responsibility for Land Lease)
  • Minister of the Interior (Education, Health, Infrastructure, Joint Responsibility for Land Lease)
  • Minister of the Exterior (Armed Forces, Foreign Relations, Aid)
  • Minister of the Law (Policing, Legal Policy, Interface to the Courts)

Current Political Status

Currently, the Executive is held by the majority party, the Progressive Alliance, a populist centrist party lead by President Jason Vernon, with 87% of the vote as of the 2020 elections, a position it has enjoyed since 1924.

The other parties are the Maldian United Socialists, a left-wing party who hold 9% of the vote, and the National Party of Malden, a right-wing party who hold the remaining 4% of the vote.

Political Issues

Despite Malden's is a de-jure democractic, it is an autocracy de-facto, with concerns raised over the independence of the judiciary (the current Chief of the Court is the brother of the President), fraud concerns raised over elections, and general Government corruption.

Even with the large amount of money flowing into Malden through military contracts, numerous areas of the island are underdeveloped and budget shortfalls are the norm, with legislation failing to implement any reforms.

Defence and Policing

Gendarmé

The Nation's primary Policing force is the Gendarmé, under the Executive Control from the Minister of the Law. However, this responsibility has been delegated to Commandant de la Force (incumbent Colonel Sokratis Antonis) for the past 27 years.

Through this, the Gendarmé enjoy pseudo-independence in most matters, though influence can be asserted through both the legislative and executive branches, through the laws enforced and the Commandant respectively. Individual Officers can be considered to be neutral to the Government itself, but bound to the orders of their superior officers.

Malden Defence Forces

The Nation's defence is handled by the Malden Defence Forces, a joint professional army and civic militia.

The majority of ongoing matters, such as training, logistics and equipment are handled by a 3 division-strength standing army, with forces bolstered by the activation of a civic militia.

The Commander in Chief of the MDF is the Minister of the Exterior de-jure, however, as with the Gendarme, responsibility has been delegated to the Marshal of the Malden Defence Forces (incumbent General Marshal Yorgos Spyros).

Current Situation

Background

Two weeks ago, violence erupted between two opposing factions:

Firstly, an anti-Government terror organisation, formed from members of the political group known as the Maldian United Socialists, with the first acts being a bomb placed under the car of the Minister of the Budget. This group mostly works in isolated cells, with support from a sector of the community, mainly from the underserved central and western areas. Unfortunately, due to the aforementioned election fraud issues, actual levels of local support are unknown.

Secondly, a pro-Government "militia" formed from members of both the Progressive Alliance and National Party of Malden, known as the Defenders of Malden, have conducted indiscriminate, retaliatory, extrajudicial killings.

Death tolls for civilians ranges into the hundreds, along with attacks on members of the government and critical infrastructure. Due to the nature of these quick raids from both sides, the Gendarme is unable to restore order. The Malden Defence Force is unable to be activated, due to the number of members of both sides who would gain ready access to firearms and armoured vehicles. Despite these issues, the membership of both Defence and Police forces have not been involved with either side of the conflict, and stated before, should be integrated into the Task Force.

Governmental Response

The Government of Malden have sent a request to both the UN and various countries directly, for assistance in restoring order and keeping the peace.

In response, the United Kingdom, United States, Czech Republic, Ireland and Canada authorised Military Aid to the Civil Power operations, combined with activation of UN peacekeepers, jointly forming Operation Iron Shield.

The Irish 2nd Engineer Group has been deployed with the US Army 304th Sustainment Brigade, constructing the Home Operating Base of Point Talon, at the town of La Riviere. 102nd Battalion are to deploy to Point Talon, to provide base security, peacekeeping forces and conducting MACP operations.

The International Development & Aid Project (IDAP) also activated a crisis team, and have set-up their presences out of a compound next to Point Talon.

Operating Environment

Malden is a tropical island, with yellow/green ground and some woodland. For this environment, MTP, OCP, or OEF-CP are recommended. Arid, Tan and Woodland may be suitable in some areas. Arctic and Black should be avoided.

Temperatures can vary from 5 to 45 degrees Celsius (41 to 113 degrees Fahrenheit), often with intense heatwaves. For this reason, camo nets are being issued, and are recommended to be used to provide shade and protection from the sun.

Logistical Support

The Island has some logistical issues, with a single airfield on the main island. Due to this, Point Talon has been established in a mid-size harbour, to allow for water-based logistical support, with supplies entering through the Azur Golf.

Maldian United Socialists

The Maldian United Socialists are a Political Party turned Terror Group, with the stated goal of improving conditions for all throughout the island and replacing the Government with one who listens to the people.

This group have been fairly successful, enjoying great amounts of popular support to the center and west of the island.

Structure

The group work in isolated cells, with little communication associated with typical militaries, focusing instead on uerilla / asymmetrical tactics, attacking targets of opportunity, initiating firefight or detonating explosives, then quickly withdrawing before overwhelming firepower can be brought to bear.

Targets

The group attempt to avoid civilian casualties, instead targeting the Government and Defenders of Malden.

Criteria for Victory

The keys to winning against this group are to build up intelligence to allow the arrest of leaders, reducing the flow of ideas and arms.

Assets Summary
  • Unknown Manpower.
  • Strong Popular Support.
  • Explosives.
  • WWII / Cold War Surplus Equipment.
Tactical Assessment
  • Current Successes come down to speed, surprise and violence of action.
  • Strong holdouts will likely be converted by improving social conditions.
  • Terroristic / Insurgent tactics will likely be dangerous to deployed forces, even if deaths of BLUEFOR aren't intended by the group.
  • The group have not objected to UN intervention, and may be open to negotiation.
  • General supplies for the group do not seem limited, likely due to strong auxiliary members / mass-base support.
Alignment

Forces are to be currently considered Neutral / OPFOR.
Further intelligence work is ongoing to clarify this.

Defenders of Malden

The Defenders of Malden are a reactionary "militia", aimed at killing the Maldian United Socialists and continuing the power of the Progressive Alliance.

This group have been less successful, but extremely violent, with indiscriminate retaliatory killings.

Structure

As with the United Socialists, this group work in isolated cells, though with a stronger leadership chain. This group tend to utilise quick attacks, though this normally compromises reprisal attacks rather than insurgent tactics.

Targets

This group make no definition of target, attacking any group it deems most advantageous at the current time.
This group have also denounced our involvement in Malden.

Criteria for Victory

Removal of the current Government could devolve this group from reprisal group to insurgency, though this goes against our objectives.

This group may dissolve if the Maldian United Socialists are defeated, though this is not certain.

Alternatives include conventional de-radicalisation, intelligence and anti-trafficking efforts.

Assets Summary
  • Unknown Manpower.
  • WWII / Cold War Equipment.
  • Some Modern Equipment.
  • Modified Civilian Vehicles.
Tactical Analysis
  • This group is a direct danger to our operations.
  • Reprisal attacks constitute a direct threat to both civilians and BLUEFOR operatives.
  • Negotiations with this group are unlikely.
Alignment

Forces are currently considered OPFOR.
