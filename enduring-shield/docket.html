Background Information

Fapovo, The Democratic Federal Republic of

Geography & Population

The Democratic Federal Republic of Fapovo (hereafter Fapovo), is an Atlantic Island Archipelago, population of 56,000, formed from the major island of Fapovo and the minor outlying islands, known as Mermaid (West), Nebo Proboj (North West), Noela, Tera, Laura and Zora (South East).

The largest outlying island is somewhat developed, mostly supported through a number of military camps and depots.

Next, Mermaid is used as a live-fire training facility, with the permanent residents being the military air base personnel at MOB Mermaid.

Finally, all other islands are either lightly developed, or un-developed.

Economy

The Republic is classed as a MEDC, with an economy mostly based around tourism and oil extraction and refining.

However, the economy is also boosted by foreign military training assets, such as short-term leases for MOB Mermaid.

Despite the MEDC status, a number of cillages on the outlying islands are mainly based around fishing and agriculture.

Political & Legal Systems

Fapovo is a constitutional democratic republic, with seperated executive, legislative and judicial branches. The Executive Branch is known as the Federal Board, the Legislative Branch as the General Assembly, and the highest court being the Court of Constitutional Appeals.

Elections for the Assembly are held every 4 years, and are generally rated highly by democracy monitors. Elections for the Federal Board are held at maxumum every 8 years, though can be called forward by either popular vote, or by a vote of no confidence by the assembly.

History

Briefly, the Islands were a British Colony, under the adminstrative control of the USA. Governmental functions were ceeded to a local government body in the aftermath of the Pig War. After Second World War, the island was under de-facto control of The USSR as a trade supply base and resupply depot. After the collapse of the USSR, the islanders voted for a new constitution.

Political Status

Currently, the island is held by a libertarian centrist coalition, though control often swings center-left through center-right between elections. The executive held by a center-left candidate, President Isaac Khan. The judicial branch however, is generally regarded as right-wing, headed by Chief Justice Gilbert Terrell.

Political Issues

Whilst typically stable, a number of separatist groups have been funded, in both the North and South of the country, promoting return to an island split between Russia and America.

National Defence, Policing, Intelligence and Civil Militia

Gendarmerie Civilé

The Nation's primary policing force is the Gendarmé, a paramilitary policing force, under the Executive Control of Director General of the Gendarmerie Civilé.

The Gendarmerie are generally noted as being competent and well-trained, however their equipment for counter-terror and counter-insurgency operations is limited, generally calling upon the Military Police for such situations.

Rangers

The Rangers are a poorly equipped paramilitary policing force, with jurisdiction outside urban areas, acting as both a sworn police force in their own right, and as a forestry service.

Due to their focus on forestry policing, and generally positive relationship with the public, weapons carried by the rangers tend to be for defence against animals, rather than people.

Despite the lack of equipment, the rangers tend to have the highest morale of all the branches.

Armed Forces of the Democratic Federal Republic of Fapovo

The defence of the Nation from external threats, and some internal security is handled by the Armed Forces, generally following the Finnish system, a professional core supplemented with mandatory conscription.

The forces generally use current or last generation equipment, with units undergoing modernisation as blocks. Post-modernised units use Geometric Woodland Camo. Pre-modernised units use various colours of French Lizard camo.

The professional core, as with the Gendarmerie, are well-trained and competent. However, conscripts are often trained to poorer standards, and are likely to suffer morale issues.

Ministries of Intelligence and Counter-Intelligence

Whilst nominally effective, the two ministries appear to be in conflict between themselves. Conflicts appear to be typically minor, but have in the past escalated to assaults and property destruction.

Current Situation

Over the past few weeks, violence greatly increased within, the two groups of seperatists, against both the opposing groups, and the government.

The government has been unable to restore order, and is fearful of tensions between the groups erupting into a full scale civil war.

Overall, intelligence reports that the death toll for civilians ranges into the thousands, along with attacks on government members, police, military members and civic infrastructure.

Government Response

The Government has requested support, in a request sent to both the UN, and to various participant nations. In response, the UN has authorized deployment of peacekeeping forces, with forces deployed from The United Kingdom, United States and Germany.

Third Party Response

The International Development and Aid Project (IDAP) have activated a crisis team, and are currently awaiting permission to enter, contingent on a secure point of operations to work from.

Deployment Information

The 304th Sustainment Brigade has set up a base of operations in the town of Bumin, henceforth referred to as Point Talon. Further FOBs will be deployed as required.

Northern Separatist Insurgency

Assets Summary
  • Cold War Surplus Weapons
  • Light Transport Vehicles
  • Explosives
Alignment

OPFOR Alpha, Opposing OPFOR Beta, INDFOR, Neutral BLUFOR

Structure

This group appear to work in isolated cells, with little typical communication, focusing on asymmetric warfare; attacking targets of opportunity, initiating firefights or detonating explosives, then quickly withdrawing before overwhelming firepower can be brought to bear.

Targets

The group attempt to avoid civilian casualties, instead focusing targets on the Government and OPFOR Beta.

Criteria for Victory

The keys to winning against this group are to build up intelligence to allow the arrest of leaders, reducing the flow of ideas and arms.

Operating Area

Whilst the group has a stronger base of support in the north; they have been seen in the south with regular occurrence, likely due to their cell based structure.

Tactical Assessment
  • Current Successes come down to speed, surprise and violence of action.
  • Strong holdouts will likely be converted by improving social conditions.
  • Terroristic / Insurgent tactics will likely be dangerous to deployed forces, even if deaths of BLUEFOR aren't intended by the group.
  • The group have not objected to UN intervention, and may be open to negotiation.
  • General supplies for the group do not seem limited, likely due to strong auxiliary members / mass-base support.

Structure

This group is structured more as a traditional militia, with a formalized rank structure. The group is more likely to engage in protracted fights, though will still utilise insurgent tactics in reprisal attacks against suspected northern sympathisers.

Targets

This group is more indiscriminate in their attacks, appearing to have no formal target, attacking any group it deems most advantageous at the current time. They have also denounced our involvement in Fapovo.

Criteria for Victory

Suggested criteria are conventional de-radicalisation, intelligence and anti-trafficking efforts. Continuing COIN to medium-long term.

Operating Area

This group solely operate in the south of the country, with no attempt to move past into the north.

Tactical Assessment

This group is a direct danger to our operations, with reprisal attacks constituting a direct threat to both civilians and BLUEFOR operatives. The immediate denouncement suggest that negotiations are also unlikely.

Administration & Logistics

Resupply

Supplies will be brought in through the 304th Sustainment Brigade to Point Talon. Further logistics is up to each field commander. Should further FOBs be set up, the 304th will resupply those, where safe to do so.

Medical

Medical Facilities are available at Point Talon, and at hospitals across the island.

Rules of Engagement

  • Nothing in these rules of engagement limits your right to take appropriate action to defend yourself and your unit.
  • You have the right to use force to defend yourself against attacks or threats of such.
  • Hostile fire may be returned effectively and promptly to stop a hostile act.
  • When JTF forces are attacked by unarmed hostile elements, mobs and/or rioters, forces should use the minimum force required, which is proportional to the threat.
  • You may not seize the property of others to accomplish your mission.
  • Detention of civilians is authorised.

Remember

  • We are not at war.
  • Treat all persons with dignity and respect.
  • Use minimum force to carry out your mission.
  • Always be prepared to act in self defence.
